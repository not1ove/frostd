01 / BRAND FOUNDATION
WHAT this is.
THE BRIEF · THE WORLD · THE RULES

FROSTD is alcoholic gelato — real spirits, frozen, served at events and drops across Mumbai. Not a café. Not a delivery brand. Not a dessert company with alcohol added.

It's a drinks brand in frozen form. Exclusive to specific events. A waitlist and a drop model. The entire experience — from the website age gate to the cup in someone's hand — is designed to communicate one thing: this is not for everyone.

The brand was built from zero by the founder. Every design decision was made to sit in the same world as CRTZ, Palace, Jacquemus, and Milk Records — not in the world of food delivery apps, generic dessert brands, or anything that needs to explain itself.

The target: Mumbai 20-somethings who already have taste. They don't need to be told something is cool. They need to feel it immediately. If a design deliverable needs explaining, it has already failed.

// FIVE CORE PRINCIPLES
01
EXCLUSIVITY OVER REACH
Every design choice reinforces scarcity. Drops only. List only. Mumbai only. Never mass-market and never designed to look like it.
02
CONFIDENCE WITHOUT EXPLANATION
The brand never explains itself. It states. It declares. The audience figures out the rest — and the act of figuring it out is part of the appeal.
03
RAW OVER POLISHED
Crayon marks. Rough edges. Intentional asymmetry. FROSTD looks made by someone with taste, not by a branding agency on a corporate brief.
04
TYPOGRAPHY IS THE DESIGN
Three fonts. Used with precision and restraint. They carry the entire visual identity. No illustration, no AI imagery, no stock photography.
05
NO PERMISSIONS ASKED
The brand's core line is also its operating principle. If a design needs approval from a committee to exist, it is not a FROSTD design.
// THE THREE WORDS
frozen.
The product. The process. The attitude. Everything FROSTD does is deliberate, controlled, cold. Never reactive. Never desperate.
spiked.
The surprise. There is alcohol in this. That is the whole point. The brand leans into it — never apologises for it, never hides it, never softens it.
drops.
The model. Not available. Not in a store. Not on delivery. You either know about it or you don't. The drop model is the entire marketing strategy.
02 / CULTURAL DNA
WHERE IT lives.
STREET · SKATE · UNDERGROUND · FROZEN LUXURY
// THE WORLD FROSTD BELONGS TO
FROSTD is not a food brand. It is a cultural brand that happens to be edible.

The reference points are streetwear drops, skate brands, underground music labels, and fashion houses that operate on scarcity and attitude. The design sensibility comes from a specific intersection: the rawness of CRTZ and skate culture meeting the frozen luxury of Jacquemus, grounded in the editorial confidence of independent print culture.

When designing for FROSTD, the question is never "does this look like a food brand?" The question is: "does this look like something Palace or CRTZ would drop?"
CRTZ
STREETWEAR · LONDON
Rules on who can buy. Drop-only. No explanation of the brand necessary. The logo is a mark of belonging, not a marketing device. Operates entirely on community and word of mouth.
FROSTD TAKES: The oval mark energy. The drop model. The attitude that not everyone gets to participate. The stamp-and-screen-print aesthetic.
JACQUEMUS
FASHION · PARIS
Bags in freezers. Products placed somewhere they shouldn't be. Unexpected contexts. Minimal but warm. The product as the protagonist — never over-styled, always slightly surreal.
FROSTD TAKES: The frozen luxury aesthetic. Product in unexpected locations. The warmth inside the minimal. The Jacquemus freezer photo is a direct FROSTD reference.
MILK RECORDS
MUSIC · INDEPENDENT
Yellow background. Hand-drawn bottle outline. Chunky rough type. Feels like a sticker slapped on something that matters. Totally unpolished. Completely confident.
FROSTD TAKES: The yellow stamp energy. The hand-drawn mark. The feeling of something that was made in a room, not a boardroom.
PALACE
SKATE · LONDON
Started as a skate crew. The logo is a triangle with a joke in it. The brand grew because real people wore it — not because of advertising. Anti-corporate by architecture.
FROSTD TAKES: The crew energy. Something that starts underground and grows because people who were there first tell people who weren't. The drop as an event.
POMBO STUDIOS
CREATIVE · PHOTOGRAPHY
Pink ice cream melting on concrete. Flash photography. Products as protagonists. Slightly grotesque. Sensory. The kind of image that makes you feel something before you understand it.
FROSTD TAKES: The product drip on concrete. The flash aesthetic. The sensory close-up. Real over staged. Overexposed over perfect.
SKATE CULTURE
SUBCULTURE · GLOBAL
No permission asked from anyone. Spots that weren't meant for skating. Made something out of nothing. The city as the canvas. Flash photography at night. Candid documentation over styled content.
FROSTD TAKES: The "no permissions asked" ethos — literally. The city as the venue. The documentation feel. Events that feel like sessions, not launches.

JetBrains Mono is the information layer. It never leads. It annotates, labels, and times-stamps everything Archivo Black declares.
RULE 01
Archivo Black leads
Every composition starts with Archivo Black. It sets the tone. Then Playfair arrives as the release. JetBrains annotates. Never reverse this hierarchy.
RULE 02
One italic moment
One Playfair italic word or short phrase per composition. If you use it twice, it loses its power. The contrast is everything.
RULE 03
Tracking is tight
Archivo Black tracks at -0.03em to -0.05em at large sizes. JetBrains Mono tracks at +0.15em to +0.30em. The contrast in tracking reinforces the hierarchy.
RULE 04
No other fonts
Three fonts. Locked. If a font feels missing, the problem is the layout not the type system. Redesign the layout, do not introduce a fourth typeface.
RULE 05
JetBrains all caps
JetBrains Mono always appears in uppercase in FROSTD contexts. It is a label, a stamp, a data point — not running copy. Never sentence case.
RULE 06
Playfair always blue
The italic moment is always #1E5BFF. Never cream. Never white. Never any of the drop accent colours. The blue italic is a signature, not a variable.

RULE 01
MAX 3 LINES
First screen before "more" is all you get. If it doesn't land in 3 lines, cut it. People won't tap more unless the first 3 lines earn it.
RULE 02
NO EXCLAMATION MARKS
Not one. Ever. They signal desperation. FROSTD is never desperate. Confidence doesn't exclaim. It states.
RULE 03
NEVER EXPLAIN THE POST
The post explains itself. The caption adds a different layer — never a description of what's already visible. If you're tempted to explain, delete it entirely.
RULE 04
NO EMOJI. EVER.
In captions, in copy, in any communication. The typography and the words carry the emotion. Emoji dilute the brand voice completely and signal mass-market thinking.
RULE 05
PERIODS ADD WEIGHT
"gone in 40 minutes." hits differently than "gone in 40 minutes" — the period makes it final, declarative, done. Use this intentionally in all short-form copy.
RULE 06
CTA IS ALWAYS FROSTD.IN
Never "link in bio." Never "@frostd.in" as a CTA. Direct to frostd.in. The site is the destination. The handle is not the call to action.

09 / HARD RULES
NEVER this.
NON-NEGOTIABLE · ALL DESIGN PARTNERS · NO EXCEPTIONS
NO GRADIENTS
On type, backgrounds, or marks. Ever. The palette is flat and intentional. Gradients read as 2019 Dribbble design. FROSTD is not that.
NO AI IMAGERY
No AI-generated photography or illustration. The crayon marks are intentional handcraft. AI-generated imagery is the opposite of what this brand stands for.
NO GLOW ON TYPE
Glow effects are for the logo mark on the website only. Never apply glow to type, body copy, or decorative elements in any print or social deliverable.
NO LIFESTYLE CLICHÉS
No stock photography of people laughing with food. No perfectly styled flat lays. FROSTD is flash photography, raw concrete, real moments. Staged is wrong.
NO EMOJI ANYWHERE
Not in copy. Not in captions. Not in any deliverable. The typography carries the emotion. Emoji dilute the brand voice completely.
NO THIRD FONT
Three typefaces. Locked. If a font feels missing, the problem is the layout not the type system. Redesign. Never introduce a fourth face.
NO FOOD AESTHETIC
No Swiggy-adjacent design. No bright chirpy colours on product shots. FROSTD is a drinks brand in frozen form — not a dessert brand with alcohol in it.
NO HANDLE ON CREATIVE
@frostd.in does not appear on social creative. frostd.in does. The URL is a destination. The handle is not the CTA.
NO EXPLAINING
No servile copy explaining what FROSTD is. The audience figures it out. If they need it explained they are not the audience.
